## Research Focus

This material describes research ideas related to community living inspired by Life Itself community, with a particular focus on communal co-living space usage. The original README dates the work to an effort that started in December 2021.

The source material frames the main question as: how can community life be documented, mapped, and analyzed in general?

The historical README also raises more concrete questions:

- What common practices can be inherited from monasteries and compared with more recent communities?
- How can intentional communities around the world be analyzed through an open database?
- What are the life-expectancy patterns of communities based on type?
- Which features or feature combinations matter for long-term survival?
- Can the average sustainability time of a community be estimated from its features?

## Method For Mapping Community Life

The historical package proposes an abstract method for creating and mapping community life across space and time.

Its example case was documentation of activities and space usage in Life Itself during Sympoesis #2, with the aim of producing a mapping technique that could later be applied to other communities.

A central concept is a community "phase space". In the original note, this is used to map activities against qualitative axes. The example axes described there are:

- `discussions` vs. `contemplation`
- `individual` vs. `collective`

The intent is to use this kind of mapping to compare community experiences across places and periods, and to better understand patterns of comfort, discomfort, and coordination in shared living environments.
